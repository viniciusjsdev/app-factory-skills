# Comparable cohort analysis

## Cohort definition

Start with job-to-be-done and audience. Add business model, geography, maturity, and platform as needed. Name direct competitors, substitutes, and adjacent examples separately.

Use explicit rules:

- inclusion: properties an app must have;
- exclusion: properties that make comparison misleading;
- observation window: same period whenever possible;
- storefront: same country or clearly separated;
- survivorship: include inactive or weak performers when the question concerns market odds.

## Distribution before anecdotes

Report sample size, missingness, median, quartiles, range, and leader concentration before highlighting individual winners. A single breakout app is not the base case.

## Developer families

Resolve publisher aliases and compare the portfolio, not just the seed app. Distinguish total portfolio scale from the concentration in the relevant app family.

## Derived metrics

Only compute a derived metric when numerator and denominator use compatible scopes and periods. `estimated revenue / estimated downloads` is a rough monetization signal, not ARPU or lifetime value.

## Scenario anchors

- conservative: lower cohort percentile or evidence-backed floor;
- base: median or most relevant matched subgroup;
- upside: upper percentile that does not depend on an extreme outlier.

State every adjustment and assumption. Do not call a scenario a forecast.
