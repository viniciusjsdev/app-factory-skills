# Commercial validation method

## Reconstruct before interpreting

Compare three states:

1. planned: contract, audience, creative, target, timing, threshold, and cost cap;
2. executed: receipt-confirmed artifact, account, URL, recipients, schedule, and actual spend;
3. observed: analytics, platform metrics, product events, CRM outcomes, and qualitative feedback.

Do not attribute results to the plan when execution differed.

## Funnel diagnosis

Use counts and denominators. Locate the first meaningful break:

- low qualified reach: audience/channel/distribution;
- reach without attention: hook/format/relevance;
- attention without click: proof/offer/CTA;
- click without signup: message match, page, friction, trust;
- signup without activation: onboarding/product value;
- activation without purchase/retention: pricing, value persistence, or wrong success metric.

This is a diagnostic hypothesis, not automatic causation. Recommend a discriminating test.

## Economics

Include complete spend and operational cost where available. Calculate cost per qualified signal at the deepest trustworthy stage. Do not extrapolate CAC/LTV from insufficient conversion or retention data.

## Qualitative evidence

Group direct feedback by recurring job, objection, confusion, trust concern, and requested outcome. Preserve counts and sampling method. Anecdotes explain possible causes; they do not replace behavioral outcomes.
