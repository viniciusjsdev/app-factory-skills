# Evidence audit checklist

## Claim traceability

- Every critical claim maps to an evidence ID or is labeled inference/assumption.
- Citations resolve to the original source where possible.
- Provider summaries preserve their underlying URLs or stable IDs.

## Source independence

- Repeated reports from one dataset or announcement share a dependence group.
- Retrieval by different tools is not counted as independent evidence.
- AppStoreTracker fields derived from Apple are not double-counted.

## Metric integrity

- Observed, modeled estimate, derived, and unknown fields are separated.
- Units, currencies, storefronts, periods, and cohorts are compatible.
- Formulas can be reproduced from recorded inputs.
- Precision matches the quality of the data.

## Coverage and bias

- Inclusion/exclusion rules are visible.
- Missingness, survivorship, selection, and availability biases are discussed.
- Counterevidence and failed searches are retained.
- Scientific discovery metadata is not treated as full-paper proof.

## Decision integrity

- The outcome follows the stated falsification policy.
- Confidence reflects the weakest critical claim, not the volume of text.
- The next experiment has a measurable pass/fail condition and stop rule.
