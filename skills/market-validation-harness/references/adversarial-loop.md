# Adversarial market-validation loop

## Principle

The loop tests whether the evidence changes a decision. It is not a debate performance and does not justify extra provider calls merely to simulate multiple agents.

## Lens outputs

### Research lead

- restated decision and hypothesis;
- evidence map by hypothesis;
- critical assumptions and missing evidence;
- current falsification status.

### Methodology reviewer

- metric-definition and sample checks;
- compatible geography, platform, and periods;
- estimate/observation separation;
- calculation reproduction;
- selection, survivorship, and confirmation bias risks.

### Devil

Build the strongest source-backed rejection case. Prefer structural risks and counterevidence over hypothetical objections.

### Angel

Build the strongest source-backed opportunity case. Acknowledge limitations and avoid using the founder's enthusiasm as evidence.

### Arbiter

Name the few decision-driving claims, determine what is actually resolved, and show why one side is stronger. Do not average incompatible conclusions.

### Evidence auditor

Check original-source access, citations, freshness, dependence groups, transformations, contradictions, and claim strength.

### Experiment designer

Choose the smallest ethical experiment that can change the decision. Define population, channel, offer/prototype, primary metric, guardrails, pass/fail threshold, duration/sample logic, cost cap, and stop rule.

### Scribe

Produce one traceable brief. Preserve minority findings and search failures rather than smoothing them away.

## Iteration

One loop is the default. Repeat only if the arbiter identifies a specific resolvable gap and the next retrieval/experiment can materially alter the outcome.
