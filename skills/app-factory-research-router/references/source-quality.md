# Source quality and freshness

## Preference order

Prefer, when appropriate:

1. primary documents, first-party listings, original datasets, original studies, and measured experiments;
2. reputable secondary analysis with transparent method;
3. provider estimates with defined methodology and limitations;
4. commentary, directories, snippets, and unattributed summaries for discovery only.

The best source depends on the claim. A company page is authoritative for its current price, not for unbiased proof of customer value.

## Evaluation fields

Assess authority, directness, method transparency, sample/coverage, recency, geographic fit, metric definition, conflicts of interest, and reproducibility.

## Freshness

Set a freshness window in the research contract. Use event date rather than only page update date. Current platform metrics must record observation time because they change continuously.

## Claims

- Cite near the claim.
- Use ranges and appropriate precision for estimates.
- Distinguish correlation from causation.
- Preserve contradicting evidence.
- Report inaccessible or failed searches rather than omitting them.
