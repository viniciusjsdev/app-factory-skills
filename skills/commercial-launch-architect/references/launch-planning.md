# Commercial launch planning

## Source hierarchy

Use, in order: approved product truth, approved research/validation if available, product evidence, brand constraints, and explicit assumptions. Never convert an assumption into a public claim.

## Launch objective

Choose a decision, not a ceremony. Examples include validating qualified signup intent, finding a repeatable creator channel, testing a price, or learning which problem frame produces activation.

## Positioning frame

Capture:

- audience and triggering context;
- desired outcome and current alternative;
- product category/reference frame;
- differentiated mechanism or benefit;
- supporting proof;
- objections and honest limitations.

## Channel selection

Score audience presence, native format fit, feedback speed, conversion path, measurement, operating effort, and cost. Start with the smallest set that can test the hypothesis.

## Campaign tasks

Every task has an owner, artifact, dependency, approval, due/launch date, metric, completion rule, and fallback. Platform write actions remain separate operations.

## Measurement

Define a primary decision metric, funnel denominators, leading indicators, guardrails, attribution window, UTM convention, reporting cadence, and pass/fail/stop thresholds before launch.

## Optional factory handoffs

- Research Factory: unresolved market or platform-native evidence;
- Marketing Creative Builder: approved content production;
- Commercial Router: approved external operation;
- Commercial Validation Analyst: post-run interpretation;
- MVP Factory: only when the user explicitly requests product work.

No handoff is automatic.
