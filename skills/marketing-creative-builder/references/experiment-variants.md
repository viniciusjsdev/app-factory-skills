# Creative experiment variants

## One decision-driving change

Prefer variants that change one meaningful dimension while holding the rest stable:

- problem-led versus outcome-led hook;
- product demonstration versus customer proof;
- specific use case versus broad category framing;
- low-friction trial CTA versus consultation CTA;
- creator-native presentation versus polished product presentation.

If multiple dimensions must change, label the test as a package comparison and do not attribute the result to one element.

## Variant record

For every variant, state hypothesis, control/baseline, changed variable, invariant elements, audience, placement, primary metric, guardrails, minimum run/sample logic, pass/fail rule, and what decision follows.

## Avoid

- color-only variants without a reasoned hypothesis;
- many variants with insufficient traffic;
- changing the landing page, offer, audience, and creative simultaneously;
- declaring a winner from platform engagement when the target is activation or purchase.
